1x2 matrix of blocks¶

The following routines horizontally merge two matrices which have consistent leading dimensions and sizes. When the matrices are distributed, their alignments must also be distributed. That is to say, if the inputs are consistent, a view is constructed of \(\begin{pmatrix} B_L & B_R \end{pmatrix}\).

Note

\(B_L\) and \(B_R\) must be contiguous in the sense that, if the local portion of \(B_L\) is \(m \times n\) with leading dimension ldim, then the top-left entry of \(B_R\) must occur precisely ldim times \(n\) entries after the top-left entry of \(B_L\), and \(B_R\) must have an equivalent leading dimension.
